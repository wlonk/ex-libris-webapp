About Ex Libris

What's this all about, anyway?

Well, I've got a lot of PDFs in my Dropbox. For the most part, they're RPG rules, but all kinds of other things find their way in there, too: fiction, histories of China, books on how to run a successful Kickstarter campaign, and more.

I wanted a better way to manage them than a bunch of folders. Something a bit more like iTunes, frankly, where I could search by author, publisher, year, or title, and actually find what I was looking for. Thus, Ex Libris.

Huh, cool. But who are you?

My name's Kit La Touche. I'm a linguist, programmer, game designer, and wannabe seamonster. I tweet sometimes, too.

Why "Ex Libris"?

So, the phrase is Latin for "from the books (of)", and is a common sight on bookplates, which are no longer common sights. They were once a way of marking books as belonging to a certain person's personal library. When I was a little kid, my parents made me a stamp in the tradition of a bookplate, that read "Ex Libris Kit La Touche" and had a picture of a polar bear reading, and I fell in love with the phrase. Years later, I decided to use it here.

You didn't do this all on your own, did you?

Absolutely not! I had and have some great help from some great people. First, the design is the work of my friend Brooke, and the logo is by her friend Zoe. I also have had some development help from my friend Austin. And I could get help from you, too, if you're interested! I'm always happy to talk with smart people.
